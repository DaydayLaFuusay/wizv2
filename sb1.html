The Tricky Nature of Phishing

Phishing is a deceptive practice that can be difficult to spot both for humans and technologies. The attackers often disguise themselves as trustworthy entities, making it challenging for individuals to distinguish between legitimate and fraudulent requests. For technologies, the constantly evolving tactics used by phishers make it hard to keep up and effectively block all phishing attempts.

Red Flags for Phishing

Here are some red flags that can help you identify phishing threats:

  • 1 Unexpected emails asking for personal information.
  • 2 Emails containing spelling and grammar mistakes.
  • 3 Emails that create a sense of urgency.
  • 4 Emails that do not address you by your name.
  • 5 Emails containing suspicious links or attachments.
  • 6 Emails from unknown senders.

Phishing Prevention Checklist

Follow this checklist to avoid falling victim to phishing attacks:

  • 1 Always verify the sender's email address.
  • 2 Never share sensitive information via email.
  • 3 Be wary of emails that create a sense of urgency.
  • 4 Always check for spelling and grammar mistakes in emails.
  • 5 Never click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown senders.
