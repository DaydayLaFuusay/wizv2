Using Tools to Stay Ahead of Threats

Utilizing tools such as Gmail, Forcepoint, and Asana can significantly aid in phishing prevention. These tools offer features like spam filtering, threat detection, and task management that can help IT and developers stay ahead of potential phishing threats.

Why It's Better to Avoid Phishing Attacks

Phishing attacks can lead to significant financial loss, data breaches, and damage to reputation. It is far more cost-effective and less disruptive to prevent these attacks than to remediate them.

Example of attack Average time to remediate
In a spear-phishing attack, an attacker may gain access to sensitive data. Remediation would involve identifying the breach, isolating affected systems, eradicating the threat, and restoring data and systems. This could take weeks or even months.
In a whaling attack, a high-level executive might be tricked into transferring funds or revealing sensitive information. Remediation would involve financial recovery, legal action, and public relations management. This could take several months to a year.
In a clone phishing attack, a legitimate message is cloned but with malicious links. Remediation would involve identifying affected users, changing credentials, and ensuring no malware remains on the system. This could take several weeks.

Given the time, cost, and potential damage involved in remediation, it is clear that prevention is the best strategy against phishing attacks.
